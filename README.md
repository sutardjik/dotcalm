# .calm

_What happens if the internet stops trying to capture attention and instead becomes a place for deliberate perception? What would the internet look like if it prioritized personal perception instead of engagement metrics?_

.calm, pronounced "dot calm," is a personal visual archive built with Gatsby, slowly mapping evolving aesthetic interests across photography, architecture, digital culture, and design. This site exists as a quiet digital space where curation, taste, and philosophical reflection live outside trends, algorithms, and commercial constraints. Inspired by the visual styles of VSCO and Pinterest, it embraces the principles of the slow web to counter the toxicity of mainstream social media. Images can be explored by clicking on any of them to view in more detail.

Over time, .calm has grown beyond a simple gallery. Its deliberate curation, iterative selection, and balance of personal and found imagery create a space that can be explored across multiple lenses — from philosophy and cultural observation to digital anthropology and media theory. What began as a personal exploration of aesthetics now serves as a reflection on the ways we perceive, curate, and interact with visual culture in the digital age, quietly asserting its relevance and presence amidst the noise.
